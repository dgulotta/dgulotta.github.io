Learning seminar on p-adic geometry (Spring 2023)

Organizer
Daniel Gulotta
Location and time
Friday 3-4:30pm in CDS 324.

We will study p-adic geometry and its applications to the local Langlands correspondence, roughly following the Berkeley lectures.

Primary references

  • [FS] L. Fargues and P. Scholze, Geometrization of the local Langlands correspondence
  • [K] K. Kedlaya, Sheaves, stacks, and shtukas
  • [Sta] Stacks Project
  • [SW] P. Scholze and J. Weinstein, Berkeley lectures on p-adic geometry
  • [W1] J. Weinstein, Arizona Winter School 2017: Adic spaces
  • [W2] J. Weinstein, Shtukas and the Langlands program: a bird’s eye view

Other useful references

  • R. Huber, Continuous valuations
  • R. Huber, A generalization of formal schemes and rigid analytic varieties
  • R. Huber, Étale cohomology of rigid analytic varieties and adic spaces
  • P. Scholze, Étale cohomology of diamonds
  • T. Wedhorn, The local Langlands correspondence for GL(n) over p-adic fields
  • T. Wedhorn, Adic Spaces
  • Geometrization of the Local Langlands program (Montreal conference notes by Tony Feng)
  • Geometrization of the Local Langlads correspondence (lectures by Laurent Fargues, notes by Sean Howe)
