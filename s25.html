References

We will cover chapters 1-9 of [BC] and chapters 1-8 of [FO] (these two references cover roughly the same material), chapters 2-13 of [SW], and some additional topics. Depending on interest, these may include [Sch1], [BS], [BL].

Notes will be posted here after each class.

Main references

  • [BC] O. Brinon and B. Conrad, CMI summer school notes on p-adic Hodge theory
  • [BL] B. Bhatt and J. Lurie, Absolute prismatic cohomology
  • [BS1] B. Bhatt and P. Scholze, Prisms and prismatic cohomology
  • [FO] J.-M. Fontaine and Y. Ouyang, Theory of p-adic Galois representations
  • [Sch1] P. Scholze, p-adic Hodge theory for rigid analytic varieties
  • [SW] P. Scholze and J. Weinstein, Berkeley lectures on p-adic geometry

Other references

  • [Bel] J. Bellaiche, An introduction to the conjecture of Bloch and Kato
  • [Ber] L. Berger, An introduction to the theory of p-adic representations
  • [Bos1] S. Bosch, Lectures on formal and rigid geometry

  • [C] P. Colmez, Une construction de BdR+
  • [F] J.-M. Fontaine, Formes différentielles et modules de Tate des variétés abéliennes sur les corps locaux
  • [H] R. Huber, Étale Cohomology of Rigid Analytic Varieties and Adic Spaces
  • [K1] K. Kedlaya, p-adic cohomology: from theory to practice
  • [K2] K. Kedlaya, New methods for (φ,Γ)-modules
  • [Mil] J. Milne, Field theory and Galois theory
  • [MW] L. Mann and A. Werner, Local systems on diamonds and p-adic vector bundles
  • [R] K. Rubin, Euler systems and Kolyvagin systems

  • [Sta] Stacks Project
  • [T] J. Tate, p-divisible groups
